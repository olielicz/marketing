# Cold Outreach Playbook — OliConnect (Social Media Managers &amp; Agencies)

Per the master calendar, this starts Week 3, Day 18, targeting 50 contacts that week, plus community posts, then a second batch of 25 in Week 4.

---

## Step 1 — Build your list (free, manual, ~1-2 hrs for 50 contacts)

Sources for finding social media managers/small agencies (all free to browse):
- **Instagram/LinkedIn bios** — search "social media manager," "SMM," "social media agency" + your target niche or city
- **Small marketing agency "About/Team" pages** — most list individual team member emails or a general contact form
- **Freelance platforms' public profiles** (Upwork, Fiverr) — many freelance social media managers list a portfolio site or contact email publicly on their profile, separate from platform messaging
- **Marketing/SMM-focused Facebook Groups and Slack/Discord communities** — check group rules first; some allow direct outreach to members who post publicly, others require posting in the group itself instead of DMing (see Step 3 below for the community-post approach, which is safer and often more effective than cold DMs in these spaces)

**Target profile:** solo social media managers and small agencies (1-5 person teams) managing multiple client accounts across platforms — they feel the "recycle top posts across accounts" pain point directly and repeatedly, unlike an in-house single-brand social hire.

Track in a spreadsheet: Name, Company/Agency, Platform found on, Contact method, Date contacted, Reply Y/N.

---

## Step 2 — Email/DM templates

### Template A — Cold intro (email)

**Subject line options:**
- "Quick question about your client posting workflow"
- "[First name] — a tool for recycling top posts across accounts"

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I noticed you manage social accounts for multiple clients — I built OliConnect specifically for that workflow: connect your accounts once, and it automatically finds your best-performing posts and recycles them across every platform, so you're not manually reformatting the same caption 4 times.

It's a one-time purchase ($29), not another monthly tool subscription. Your login credentials stay in your own browser session — nothing gets stored on our end.

Worth a quick look? [landing page link]

Happy to answer anything directly.

Best,
[Your name]

---

### Template B — Shorter DM-style variant (for LinkedIn/Instagram DMs)

**Body:**

Hey [First Name] — saw you manage social for a few clients. Built a tool that auto-recycles your best posts across platforms so you're not manually reposting the same thing 4 times. One-time payment, not a subscription. [link] — no worries if not useful, just thought it might save you time.

## Step 3 — Community posts (often more effective than cold DMs for this audience)

Post directly in relevant free communities, framed as "here's a tool I built, here's the problem it solves" — NOT a bare link drop. Value-first framing performs significantly better than an ad-style post in every community listed below.

**Where to post:**
- r/socialmedia
- r/marketing
- r/smallbusiness
- Relevant Facebook Groups for social media managers/freelancers (check each group's self-promotion rules first — many have a designated weekly "share your tool/business" thread)
- Relevant Discord/Slack communities for marketers (same rule — check for a self-promo channel)

**Post template:**

> Built a tool for anyone managing social accounts across multiple platforms/clients — [OliConnect] connects all your accounts once, finds your best-performing posts automatically, and recycles them across every platform so you're not manually reformatting the same caption 4+ times.
>
> One-time payment ($29), not another subscription. Would love feedback from anyone who manages multiple accounts — what's your current process for reposting top content across platforms?

Ending with a genuine question (not just a pitch) significantly increases reply/comment engagement in these communities.

---

## Step 4 — Follow-up sequence (non-responders only, once, 5-7 days later)

**Subject line:** "Following up — OliConnect link"

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Just floating this back up in case it got buried: [landing page link]

No pressure either way — happy to answer anything if useful.

[Your name]

**Do not send more than one follow-up.**

---

## Daily execution cadence (Week 3, Days 18-21)

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| 18 | Build list of 25-30 contacts. Send Template A to first 15. Post in r/socialmedia + r/marketing. |
| 19 | Send Template A/B (split test) to next 15-20. Post in r/smallbusiness + any relevant Facebook/Discord communities. |
| 20 | Continue outreach to reach 50 total for the week. Monitor and reply to all community post comments. |
| 21 | Log replies + community engagement, note which template/post framing performed better. |

**Week 4 (Day 27):** repeat with a fresh batch of 25 contacts, using whichever approach performed best in Week 3.

---

## What counts as a "win" at this stage

- **Reply/comment engagement rate** matters more than raw outreach volume for this audience — a well-placed community post that gets 10 genuine comments is often more valuable than 50 cold DMs that get 2 replies.
- A 5-10% reply rate on cold outreach and meaningful engagement (not just upvotes) on community posts are reasonable, honest benchmarks for a new product with no existing reputation.
