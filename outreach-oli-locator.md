# Cold Outreach Playbook — Oli-Locator (Real Estate Agents)

**Why cold outreach instead of Product Hunt/directories:** Real estate agents don't browse SaaS discovery platforms looking for tools — they respond to direct, relevant outreach and referrals. This is a relationship-driven buying motion, not a discovery-driven one. Per the master calendar, this starts Week 3, Day 17, targeting 100 agents by end of week, then a second batch of 50 in Week 4.

---

## Step 1 — Build your list (free, manual, ~1-2 hrs for 50-100 contacts)

Sources for finding solo agents/small teams (all free to browse):
- **State/regional Realtor association member directories** — most are public, searchable by city
- **Zillow agent profiles** — search by city, most list an email or a "contact agent" form (use the form if no direct email is visible — this is legitimate direct outreach to a business's own listed contact channel, not scraped/purchased data)
- **LinkedIn** — search "real estate agent" + your target city; many list emails in their About section or accept LinkedIn messages
- **Local brokerage "our agents" pages** — small/independent brokerages often list every agent's individual email

**Keep this ethical and effective:** target solo agents and small teams (2-10 agents), not large national brokerages — they're the actual buyer for a tool like this, and they're far more likely to reply to a personal, non-corporate email.

Track in a simple spreadsheet: Name, Brokerage, City, Email/Contact method, Date contacted, Reply Y/N, Follow-up date.

---

## Step 2 — Email templates

### Template A — Cold intro (first contact)

**Subject line options:**
- "Quick question about your lead follow-up process"
- "[First name] — a tool for solo agents in [city]"
- "Saw you're at [brokerage] — quick question"

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I noticed you're working [city/area] independently — I built a tool called Oli-Locator specifically for agents like you who don't have a big team's budget for enterprise CRM software but still need leads, property search, a shared inbox, and call tracking in one place.

Most solo agents I talk to are juggling 3-4 separate apps for this (a lead form here, a CRM there, a separate phone system). Oli-Locator puts it all in one dashboard.

There's a free demo account you can try right now, no signup needed: [demo login link]

Worth a 10-minute look? Happy to answer any questions directly, or you can just poke around the demo yourself.

Best,
[Your name]

---

### Template B — Shorter/more casual variant (A/B test this against Template A)

**Subject line:** "Built this for agents like you — 2 min look?"

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I built a tool for independent real estate agents — leads, property search, inbox, and call tracking, all in one app instead of 3-4 separate subscriptions.

Free demo, no signup: [demo login link]

If it's not useful, no worries at all — just thought it might save you some time. Happy to answer anything if you have questions.

[Your name]

---

## Step 3 — Follow-up sequence (only for non-responders, send once, 5-7 days later)

**Subject line:** "Following up — quick demo link"

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Just floating this back up in case it got buried — here's that free demo link for Oli-Locator again: [demo login link]

No pressure either way, just wanted to make sure it didn't get lost.

[Your name]

**Do not send more than one follow-up.** A single polite follow-up is normal and expected; repeated follow-ups after silence reads as spam and damages your sender reputation for future outreach.

---

## Step 4 — Daily execution cadence (Week 3, Days 17-21)

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| 17 | Build list of 40-50 contacts. Send Template A to first 20. |
| 18 | Send Template A/B (split test) to next 20. |
| 19 | Send remaining contacts to reach 100 total for the week. Begin sending Step 3 follow-ups to Day 17's non-responders (5-7 day mark starts after this week). |
| 20-21 | Continue outreach cadence, log replies, note which subject line/template performed better. |

**Week 4 (Day 27):** repeat with a fresh batch of 50 contacts, using whichever template/subject line got the best reply rate in Week 3.

---

## Sending mechanics (free tools)

- **Volume:** 20 emails/day from a normal Gmail/Google Workspace account stays well within free sending limits and avoids spam-filter flags that bulk-sending tools can trigger
- **Personalization:** always use the actual first name and city/brokerage — generic mail-merge blasts get lower reply rates and are easier to flag as spam
- **Tracking replies:** a simple spreadsheet is enough at this volume; no paid CRM needed for 100-150 contacts/month

## What counts as a "win" at this stage

- **Reply rate** is the metric that matters most in Week 3's checkpoint, not opens. A 5-10% reply rate on cold outreach to a well-targeted list is a reasonable, honest benchmark for a new product with no existing reputation — don't expect higher on the first attempt.
- Even a "not interested, but here's someone who might want this" reply is valuable — always ask for a referral in your reply if someone declines politely.
